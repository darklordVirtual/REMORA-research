Step 2
Three-layer verification pipeline
Each citation is verified independently through three orthogonal checks
1
DCE Knowledge Base
Direct lookup in the DCE legal database (Cloudflare D1 + Vectorize).

Contains Norwegian statute law and a selection of court decisions.

Result: NOT FOUND = deterministic signal
2
Multi-Oracle Consensus
Three independent LLMs (Groq LLaMA 8B, 70B + Mistral 7B) are each asked for specific case details.

Real cases → consistent answers.
Fake cases → no consensus.

Result: CANNOT VERIFY = hallucination signal
3
Legal Principle Check
The legal principle attributed to the citation is verified against Norwegian statute law.

Even if a citation exists, the principle it is claimed to support may be incorrect.

Result: INCORRECT = content hallucination

The core principle
REMORA prefers
calibrated uncertainty
over confident hallucination
When a citation cannot be verified, the system returns
CANNOT VERIFY — not "70 % likely correct."
In legal proceedings, an honest "not found"
is more valuable than a confident wrong answer.
