# REMORA Commercial Packaging

How to make money from REMORA without pretending to be a funded startup. The
credible shape here is **open-source core + a boutique expert behind it** (solo
founder / small company), not a SaaS with a pricing page it cannot staff.

**Guiding rule:** the open-source core must stay genuinely useful on its own.
Commercial offers sell *time, assurance, and integration* — never a crippled core.

---

## The boundary

| Open source (Apache-2.0, free, forever) | Commercial (paid) |
|---|---|
| Decision engine, four-outcome gate, policy hard-blocks | Hosted Control Room (managed) |
| DecisionEnvelope + SHA-256 audit chain | Enterprise policy packs (sector rules) |
| Shadow Mode / replay | Compliance/audit evidence packages |
| MCP / LangGraph / OpenAI adapters | External validation engagement |
| Benchmarks + paper + math supplement | Governance advisory / integration |
| AROMER experimental learning layer | Managed deployment + support SLA |

The GO-STAR / cyber-offensive extension stays **out of the public core** and is
offered only under explicit engagement scope — never marketed as a feature of the
open-source repo.

## Offers

Each: buyer / pain / promise / deliverable / price anchor / risk / proof / CTA.

### 1. External validation engagement
- **Buyer:** a team adopting agentic AI that needs an independent governance read.
- **Pain:** "we can't tell if our agent guardrails actually stop unsafe actions."
- **Promise:** a reproducible, written assessment of their agent's action-safety
  posture using REMORA's Shadow Mode and benchmark methodology.
- **Deliverable:** a report (claim → evidence → caveat), a shadow-replay run on
  their action logs, and a prioritized findings list.
- **Price anchor:** fixed-fee project (multi-day). Anchor on a 1–2 week scope.
- **Risk:** scope creep. **Proof needed:** the public benchmarks + `NEGATIVE_RESULTS.md`.
- **CTA:** "Book an external validation."

### 2. Governance advisory / integration
- **Buyer:** eng leaders wiring agents into production who want it governed.
- **Pain:** "we need a pre-execution gate, but building/validating policy is not
  our core work."
- **Promise:** REMORA integrated into their agent stack with sector-appropriate
  policy and an audit trail their risk team accepts.
- **Deliverable:** working integration (MCP/LangGraph/OpenAI), policy pack, runbook.
- **Price anchor:** retainer or fixed integration project.
- **CTA:** "Discuss an integration."

### 3. Enterprise policy packs
- **Buyer:** regulated sectors (finance, energy, healthcare) deploying agents.
- **Pain:** generic policy does not map to their controls/regulations.
- **Promise:** vetted policy bundles mapped to their control framework.
- **Deliverable:** policy pack + mapping doc (e.g., to EU AI Act / NIST AI RMF).
- **Price anchor:** per-pack license or part of an engagement.
- **CTA:** "Request a sector policy pack."

### 4. Hosted Control Room (later)
- **Buyer:** teams that want the dashboard/audit without running infra.
- **Promise:** managed Control Room + retained audit trail.
- **Price anchor:** monthly per-environment. **Do not launch until there is pull.**
- **CTA:** "Join the hosted waitlist."

### 5. Workshops / training
- **Buyer:** platform/AI teams skilling up on agent governance.
- **Deliverable:** a half-day workshop on pre-execution governance + the
  DecisionEnvelope model.
- **CTA:** "Book a governance workshop."

## What to avoid
- A pricing page with tiers you cannot deliver.
- "Enterprise-grade" / "SOC 2" language before it is true.
- Gating the core behind a license. The stars come from the free core; the revenue
  comes from the expert behind it.
